Meet The Founder:

Power to Become was born from my own story.
I grew up in Mwingi, Kitui County, where dreams were big but guidance was limited. Like many rural youth, I wanted more for my future, but I didn't know where to start or who to ask.

Everything changed when I moved to Nairobi to join Multimedia University of Kenya. The transition was overwhelming. What many young people from urban areas seemed to understand naturally, such as navigating the city, choosing a career path, building networks, and growing in confidence, felt completely foreign to me. I faced culture shock, confusion, and moments of deep self-doubt.

I lacked a mentor,someone who could show me the ropes, challenge me, encourage me, and help me discover my potential. I had to learn through struggle, and I knew it didn't have to be that way.
Those early challenges became my purpose.
After years of mentoring young people in schools, churches, and community programs, I realized just how many rural youth face the same hurdles I did. That is why I founded Power to Become to bridge the gap, offer guidance, and equip young people with the life skills, confidence, and mentorship they need to thrive. Today, Power to Become exists so that no young person has to walk their journey alone.
